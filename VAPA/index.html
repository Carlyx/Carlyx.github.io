VAPA: Realistic Video-based Human Avatar by Personalized Adaptation of Video Diffusion Model

Youxin Pang1 Ruizhi Shao1 Zhaoqi Su1 Yuxiang Zhang1 Xiao Zhou1 Hongjiang Xiao2 Hongwen Zhang3 Yebin Liu1

1 Tsinghua University       2 Communication University of China       3 Beijing Normal University
Paper Code

Abstract

In this paper, we propose VAPA, a method to create a realistic video-based human avatar from a single video. The key idea of VAPA is to integrate 3D human avatar with a large pre-trained video model such as video diffusion model (VDM) and adapt it to a given video. By representing the avatar via a large video model, our solution can handle the long-standing jitter issues more easily while leveraging general human priors to improve generalization. Meanwhile, the personalized adaptation enables our model to preserve fine-grained details as much as possible in the given video. To further enhance the ability of VDM to capture motion-related details, we introduce a 3D latent semantic avatar representation and a motion-guide attention that incorporates human semantic information in 4D space, such that it can enhance the dynamic consistency between motions and appearances. Moreover, to better preserve the facial expression and identity, we also introduce an expression-aware face enhancement module with progressive fusion method to achieve fine-grained control of faces. Evaluations demonstrate our method can generate human videos with arbitrary length that exhibit coherence, realism and accuracy, while handling challenging motions in the wild.
